Common Symptoms of Kidney Issues:
  • Swelling in feet, ankles, or face
  • Fatigue and difficulty concentrating
  • Frequent urination, especially at night
  • Foamy or blood-tinged urine
  • Persistent high blood pressure
  • Loss of appetite and nausea
  • Shortness of breath and muscle cramps
Precautions & Kidney Care Tips:
  • Stay hydrated with the right amount of fluid
  • Limit salt, processed foods, and high-protein intake
  • Control diabetes and blood pressure regularly
  • Avoid overuse of painkillers and self-medication
  • Get routine urine and blood tests to monitor kidney health
  • Do not skip dialysis or medications if prescribed
  • Consult your nephrologist promptly if symptoms arise

Frequently Asked Questions

You May Ask

What are the common signs of kidney problems?
Common signs include swelling in the legs or feet, fatigue, frequent urination, blood in urine, and high blood pressure. It's important to get evaluated early.
Do you offer treatment for chronic kidney disease (CKD)?
Yes, we provide specialized care for CKD, including dietary management, medication, and ongoing monitoring to slow disease progression.
What tests are done to evaluate kidney function?
Tests include blood tests like serum creatinine, GFR (glomerular filtration rate), urine analysis, and imaging like ultrasound or CT scan.
Can kidney disease be prevented?
Yes, managing diabetes, controlling blood pressure, staying hydrated, and regular health checkups can help prevent kidney problems.
What treatments are available for kidney failure?
Depending on the stage, treatment options include medications, dialysis, or referral for kidney transplant when necessary.
